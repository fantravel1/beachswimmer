Not All Beaches Are Swimmable

A beach can be famous and still be too rough, too cold, too rocky, too dirty, or too dangerous to actually swim in. BeachSwimmer exists to tell you the truth: yes, you can actually swim here — and it's incredible.

We evaluate every beach on what matters to swimmers: water clarity, safe entry, depth, temperature, currents, and real conditions — not beauty contests or hotel reviews.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is SwimScore™?

SwimScore™ is our proprietary 1 to 100 rating system for beach swimming quality. It evaluates water clarity, safety, entry ease, depth quality, temperature comfort, crowding, cleanliness, marine life, and wave conditions to give every beach a single, honest swim rating.

What makes BeachSwimmer different from other beach websites?

BeachSwimmer is swimming-first. Most beach sites rank beauty, luxury, or popularity. We rate whether you can actually get in the water, swim safely, and enjoy it. A beach can be famous and still score low if it's too rough, rocky, or dangerous to swim.

How do you determine if a beach is swimmable?

A beach is swimmable when a person can reasonably enter, move, float, and enjoy the water. We assess rip current risk, surf behavior, entry conditions, water clarity, depth profile, and seasonal hazards to determine real swimmability.

Are BeachSwimmer ratings updated seasonally?

Yes. Swim conditions change with seasons — wind patterns shift, water temperatures change, and clarity varies with rainfall. We update SwimScore™ ratings to reflect the best and worst months for each beach.

Can I use BeachSwimmer to plan a swimming trip?

Every beach page includes nearest airports, drive times, ferry routes, and swim-first accommodation recommendations so you can plan a trip around real swimming, not just hotel pools.
