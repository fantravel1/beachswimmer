The Clean Water List

The world's cleanest, clearest swimming beaches. Crystal visibility, minimal runoff, protected coves, and exceptional underwater clarity. This list is curated carefully — it is a trust asset.

Selection Criteria

Every beach on this list meets strict clean water standards:

  • Visibility: Minimum 15 meters underwater visibility in normal conditions
  • Runoff: Minimal freshwater discharge, no industrial or agricultural drainage
  • Protection: Sheltered from swell that stirs up sediment
  • Consistency: Clear water at least 8 months of the year
  • Testing: Meets or exceeds EU/WHO bathing water quality standards where applicable

The List

  1. 1. Elafonissi

    Crete, Greece

    SwimScore™ 92

    Pink-sand lagoon with turquoise water so clear you see your shadow on the bottom. Minimal runoff — dry limestone terrain with no rivers nearby. Visibility exceeds 20 meters on calm days.

  2. 2. Cala Varques

    Mallorca, Spain

    SwimScore™ 88

    Sheltered east-coast cove with 15–25 meter visibility. Limestone cliffs filter groundwater, no urban drainage. The underwater caves are visible from the surface on calm mornings.

  3. 3. Navagio (Shipwreck Beach)

    Zakynthos, Greece

    SwimScore™ 84

    Enclosed cove with electric-blue water. Accessible only by boat, which keeps the water pristine. The limestone cliffs create a natural amphitheater. Visibility routinely exceeds 25 meters.

  4. 4. Cala Mariolu

    Sardinia, Italy

    SwimScore™ 91

    Marble pebble beach in the Gulf of Orosei. Boat-access only keeps crowds low and water untouched. Turquoise-to-sapphire gradient with 30+ meter visibility. One of the clearest swims in the Mediterranean.

  5. 5. Sarakiniko

    Milos, Greece

    SwimScore™ 85

    White volcanic rock and deep sapphire water. No sand to stir — the rocky bottom keeps visibility constant. Water is crystal-clear year-round. Cliff entry only.

  6. 6. Cala Goloritzé

    Sardinia, Italy

    SwimScore™ 90

    A natural monument. Spring-fed water emerges cold and impossibly clear at the base of a limestone cliff. Visibility exceeds 30 meters. Requires a 1.5-hour hike or boat access — the effort filters crowds.

  7. 7. Playa de Ses Illetes

    Formentera, Spain

    SwimScore™ 89

    Caribbean-grade turquoise water in the Mediterranean. Protected Posidonia sea grass meadows keep the water clear and oxygenated. Shallow, warm, and consistently crystal.

  8. 8. Rabbit Beach (Spiaggia dei Conigli)

    Lampedusa, Italy

    SwimScore™ 88

    Protected nature reserve. The water is transparent — boats appear to hover. Fine sand bottom, turtle nesting site, and strict conservation rules that protect water quality.

  9. 9. Anse Source d'Argent

    La Digue, Seychelles

    SwimScore™ 86

    Granite boulder beach with shallow turquoise lagoon. Reef-protected, minimal runoff from the tiny island. Water clarity is exceptional year-round, with visibility of 15–20 meters in the swim zone.

  10. 10. Whitehaven Beach

    Whitsunday Islands, Australia

    SwimScore™ 87

    98% pure silica sand that doesn't retain heat or stir easily. The water stays clear even in light chop. Seven kilometers of white sand with no development. Access by boat or seaplane only.

What Makes Water Clear

Clean water beaches share common traits:

  • Limestone or volcanic coastlines — natural filtration of groundwater
  • No nearby rivers — rivers carry sediment, agricultural runoff, and nutrients that cause algae
  • Protected coves — sheltered from swell that churns up the seabed
  • Limited boat traffic — reduces fuel residue and anchor damage
  • Conservation protection — marine reserves maintain water quality
  • Posidonia sea grass — filters water and stabilizes the seabed (Mediterranean)

Clean Water FAQ

What makes a beach have clean, clear water?

Clean water beaches share several traits: minimal freshwater runoff and urban discharge, sandy or rocky bottoms that don't churn sediment, protection from open-ocean swell, low boat traffic, and distance from agricultural land. Limestone and volcanic coastlines tend to have the clearest water because they filter groundwater naturally.

Where is the clearest swimming water in the world?

The Mediterranean — particularly Greece, Sardinia, and the Balearic Islands — consistently has the clearest swimming water, with visibility reaching 30+ meters. The Seychelles, parts of the Caribbean, and Western Australia also rank among the clearest.

Does rain affect water clarity at beaches?

Yes. Rain creates runoff that carries sediment, soil, and pollutants into the sea. Beaches near rivers, agricultural land, or urban areas are most affected. After heavy rain, wait 24–48 hours for clarity to recover. Remote coves and island beaches recover faster.
