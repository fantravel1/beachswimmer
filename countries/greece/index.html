Best Swimming Beaches in Greece

Crystal-clear Mediterranean water, safe sand entries, warm temperatures from June through October. Greece is a swimmer's paradise — if you know which beaches to swim at.

Why Greece for Swimming

Greece has over 13,000 km of coastline, thousands of islands, and some of the clearest water in the Mediterranean. But not every Greek beach is a good swim. Many are rocky, wind-exposed, or too shallow to swim properly.

BeachSwimmer rates every Greek beach by what matters: water clarity, safe entry, depth, temperature, and real conditions. Below are the top swimming regions and beaches, rated by SwimScore™.

Top Swimming Regions

Crete — West Coast

Elafonissi, Balos, Falassarna

Pink-sand lagoons, sheltered coves, and turquoise shallows. West Crete has the best family and lagoon swims in the country.

Cyclades

Milos, Koufonisia, Naxos

Volcanic coves with jewel-tone water, dramatic cliff entries, and snorkel opportunities. Wind-sensitive — check Meltemi forecasts.

Ionian Islands

Lefkada, Kefalonia, Corfu

Calm, deep-blue bays protected from the Meltemi. Lefkada's Porto Katsiki and Kefalonia's Myrtos are bucket-list swims.

Peloponnese

Elafonisos, Voidokilia, Simos

Mainland Greece's best swimming — uncrowded, warm, with soft sand entries and shallow wading. Simos Beach on Elafonisos is one of the best lagoon swims in Europe.

Dodecanese

Rhodes, Karpathos, Tilos

Warm, long-season swimming with fewer crowds than the Cyclades. Mix of sand and pebble entries, excellent clarity.

Northeast Aegean

Thasos, Samothraki

Pine-backed beaches with marble sand and emerald water. Cooler season, uncrowded swims with exceptional natural beauty.

Greece Swimming FAQ

What is the best month to swim in Greece?

June through September offers the warmest water (24–27°C) and calmest conditions. Late June and September are the sweet spot: warm water, fewer crowds, and lighter winds compared to the peak Meltemi season in July and August.

Which Greek island has the best swimming beaches?

Crete has the widest variety of swimmable beaches — from the pink-sand lagoon at Elafonissi to the palm-lined bay at Vai. For calm, crystal-clear cove swims, Milos and Koufonisia are hard to beat. For snorkel swims, Zakynthos and Alonissos offer exceptional clarity.

Is it safe to swim at Greek beaches?

Most Greek beaches are safe for swimming, with calm Mediterranean water and gradual entries. The main risks are wind-generated waves during Meltemi season (July–August), occasional sea urchins on rocky entries, and rare jellyfish. Always check each beach's SwimScore™ safety rating.

What is the Meltemi and how does it affect swimming?

The Meltemi is a strong northerly wind that blows across the Aegean Sea mainly in July and August. It can make north-facing beaches rough and choppy. South-facing and sheltered coves remain calm. Check wind exposure before choosing a beach during these months.
