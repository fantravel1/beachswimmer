Best Swimming Beaches in Mallorca

Turquoise calas, sand entries, warm water from May to October. Mallorca's sheltered coves are among the best swimming spots in the Mediterranean — if you pick the right coast and season.

Mallorca Swimming Overview

Mallorca's coastline is defined by calas — small, cliff-backed coves with calm, clear water. The east coast has the most sheltered swimming, while the north coast faces bigger swells and wind. The south coast offers long sandy beaches with shallow wading.

Water visibility regularly exceeds 20 meters in sheltered calas. Entry is typically sand or smooth pebble with gradual depth. Water temperature peaks at 27°C in August.

Swimming by Coast

East Coast

Cala Varques, Cala Mondragó, Cala Millor

The best swimming coast. Sheltered calas with crystal water, sand entries, and minimal wind exposure. Best for families and snorkel swims.

South Coast

Es Trenc, Cala Pi, Es Carbó

Long sandy beaches, shallow wading, warm water. Less dramatic than the calas but consistently safe and swimmable. Es Trenc is Mallorca's best open beach swim.

North Coast

Cala Sant Vicenç, Formentor, Cala Barques

Dramatic settings with deeper water and more exposure. Check wind before swimming — Tramontana can create rough conditions. On calm days, the water clarity is extraordinary.

West Coast (Serra de Tramuntana)

Sa Calobra, Cala Deià, Cala Tuent

Rocky entries, deep water, dramatic cliff settings. For experienced swimmers. The approach roads are steep and narrow. Worth it for the crystal-clear, deep-blue swims.

Mallorca Swimming FAQ

What is the best beach for swimming in Mallorca?

Cala Varques on the east coast is one of Mallorca's highest-rated swimming beaches with a SwimScore™ of 88. The sheltered cove has crystal-clear water, sand entry, and calm conditions most of the year. For families, Es Trenc on the south coast offers a long, shallow swim with warm water.

When is the best time to swim in Mallorca?

May through October offers warm swimming water (20–27°C). June and September are the best months — warm water, uncrowded beaches, and stable conditions. July and August are warmest but peak tourist season.

Are Mallorca beaches safe for swimming?

Most Mallorca calas are very safe for swimming — sheltered coves with calm, clear water and sand or pebble entries. The east coast calas are the most protected. North coast beaches can get rough with northerly winds. Always check flag conditions at lifeguarded beaches.

Which Mallorca beaches are best for snorkeling?

Cala Varques, Cala Mondragó, and Cala Figuera offer the best snorkel swims. Clear water, rocky edges with fish, and calm conditions. Bring your own gear — rental is limited at remote calas.
