Plage de Saleccia

Wild, pristine, and remote — one of Corsica's most unspoiled white-sand beaches.

89 SwimScore™

The Swim

Saleccia is a 1.5 km stretch of pristine white sand meeting crystal-clear turquoise water, accessible only by boat or hiking trail (3 hours from nearest car park). The remoteness is the feature: minimal crowds, no development, and water quality that reflects Corsica's wild character.

The beach shelves gradually and is protected from major swells. Swimming season runs May through September. The hike is moderate but rewarding for swimmers seeking genuine isolation.

Why Plage de Saleccia Matters

True Wilderness Swimming

This is not a destination beach — it's an expedition. The effort to reach it filters out casual visitors, leaving only committed swimmers and hikers. On most days, you'll have the beach largely to yourself.

Pristine Water Quality

The isolation means zero commercial impact. No jet skis, no parasailing, no runoff. Water tests show consistently high purity scores.

Dramatic Coastline

Red-granite cliffs, endemic plants, and the sense of swimming on an untamed coast make this one of the Mediterranean's most atmospheric beach experiences.

No Amenities

This is a feature, not a bug. Bring everything: water, snacks, sun protection, towels. The reward is complete solitude.

Getting There

Nearest Airport: Bastia (BIA) — 60 km away, 75 minutes by car

Option 1 — Hiking (Recommended): Park at Macinaggio village car park. Follow the marked trail north along the coast for 3 hours to reach Saleccia. Return the same way.

Option 2 — Boat: Macinaggio has summer boat services (about €20/person round trip). Boats run May through September, weather permitting.

Best Time: June through August for warmest water. July and August can attract crowds via boat, so June and early September are ideal.
