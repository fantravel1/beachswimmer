Crete, Greece

Balos Lagoon

90 SwimScore™
out of 100
Calm Lagoon Swim Family Swim

Stunning turquoise lagoon with soft sand entry and impossibly warm water — best in morning light and calm conditions.

The Swim

Balos is a vast turquoise lagoon backing a long white-sand beach, with mountains rising dramatically behind. The water is warm, clear, and shallow — you can walk waist-deep for 200+ meters. The lagoon opens to the northwest, with Gramvoussa Island visible across the water.

This is a wade-and-float experience, not a deep-water swim. The lagoon reaches its deepest point (3–4 meters) only in the center. For swimmers wanting actual depth, the outer edges near the island entrance drop to 5–6 meters within 100 meters of shore.

Best for

  • Families with children
  • Swimmers who value warmth and calm over depth
  • Photographers and sunset lovers
  • Anyone wanting an iconic Mediterranean lagoon experience

Safety Reality

  • Rip current risk: Very low in the shallow lagoon. The opening to the sea can have mild currents in windy conditions.
  • Surf behavior: Lagoon is nearly flat. The open side can develop light swell with westerly wind.
  • Hazards: No rocks, no sea urchins in the main lagoon. Occasional jellyfish in late August (rare). Watch for boats and tour groups in peak season.
  • Lifeguards: Present during summer season (June–September) in the main beach area.

Overall safety: high. This is one of the safest beach swims in Crete.

Entry Type

Sand entry. Fine white sand — perfectly flat and gradual. You can walk in waist-deep for 100+ meters without reaching chest height. No rocks, no sudden drop-offs, no special footwear needed.

Difficulty: Beginner-friendly. Safe for toddlers, elderly swimmers, and anyone nervous about sudden depth changes.

Depth Quality

The lagoon is predominantly shallow — knee to chest deep across most of the area. This is a wading and floating experience. For deeper swimming, swim toward the western opening where the lagoon transitions to open water and depth increases gradually.

Smooth sand throughout — good for all swimmers, but not ideal for competitive lap swimming.

The shallow water heats rapidly — on calm August days, the lagoon feels like a warm bath. No cold shock at any point during swim season.

Swim Weather

  • Wind direction: Balos opens northwest. Westerly and northwesterly winds can push chop into the lagoon. Easterly and southeasterly winds leave it glass-flat.
  • Swell: The lagoon is partly protected by the landscape. Large Atlantic swell occasionally wraps around in winter, but this is a summer destination.
  • Tide: Minimal tidal range (<30cm). Not a factor.
  • Clarity: Excellent on calm mornings. Wind stirs fine sand, reducing visibility from crystal to milky-blue.
  • Rain runoff: The area is semi-arid. Minimal impact. Clarity recovers quickly.

Key rule: Early morning swims are calmest and clearest. Plan to arrive by 8am in peak season to beat crowds and wind.

Best Months to Swim

June and September are ideal. Warm water, favorable wind patterns, and lower crowds than July-August. Late May can work if you tolerate slightly cooler water (20–22°C).

Crowding and Vibe

Balos is famous and accessible, so it attracts crowds. July and August bring tour buses, boat tours, and day-trippers. The beach fills by 10am. Arriving before 9am is essential in peak season.

The lagoon is large enough that crowds disperse — walk to the far ends to find quieter patches. June and September are significantly less crowded.

Vibe: Family-oriented, touristy in peak season, naturally beautiful. Mix of tourists, families, and day-trippers. The iconic scenery draws photographers and sunset watchers.

Family friendly: Yes — excellent for families.

Snorkel Potential

Limited. The lagoon is too shallow for snorkeling in most areas (you'll see bottom in 1–2 meters everywhere). For snorkeling, swim toward the deeper western opening or take a boat to explore around Gramvoussa Island.

  • Visibility: Excellent in shallow areas, but depth is minimal
  • Fish presence: Few in the lagoon; more at deeper edges and around the island
  • Hazards: None in the shallow lagoon

Who It's For / Who Should Avoid

Best for

  • Families with young children
  • Warm-water swimmers and floaters
  • Photographers seeking iconic Mediterranean scenery
  • Tourists wanting a famous, beautiful swim
  • Anyone nervous about depth

Not ideal for

  • Lap swimmers or deep-water swimmers
  • Snorkelers (too shallow)
  • Crowd-averse swimmers in July–August
  • Swimmers seeking solitude

Getting There

  • Nearest airport: Chania (CHQ) — 60 km, approximately 1.5-hour drive
  • Drive from Chania: Northwest toward Kaliviani, then follow signs to Balos. Winding mountain road with unpaved sections (passable by standard car). Allow 1.5 hours.
  • Boat tours: Daily boat trips from Kissamos harbor (30 minutes by boat). Good option if you don't want to drive the mountain road.
  • Public transport: Limited. Summer minibus service from Chania to Kaliviani, but not to the beach itself.
  • Parking: Large free lot, but fills by 10am in July–August. Overflow parking along the road.

Where to Stay (Swim-First)

  • Kaliviani (10 km): Small village on the way to Balos. Limited accommodation. Peaceful base.
  • Kissamos / Kastelli (20 km): Harbor town with restaurants, shops, and more accommodation options. Good base for boat access to Balos.
  • Paleochora (1 hour): Larger town with more amenities. Good for exploring multiple west Crete beaches.
  • Chania (1.5 hours): Major city with extensive accommodation and dining. Consider a day trip to Balos.

Swim-first tip: Kissamos offers the best balance — close enough for early arrival, with boat tour alternatives and other beach options nearby.

Balos Lagoon Swimming FAQ

Is Balos Lagoon good for swimming?

Balos is one of Greece's most stunning swimming lagoons. The water is turquoise, warm, and shallow across most of the lagoon. Soft sand entry and calm conditions make it excellent for all swimming abilities. SwimScore™: 90/100.

Is Balos Lagoon safe for families?

Yes. The lagoon is extremely shallow (knee to waist deep) with soft sand bottom and minimal current. It is one of the safest beach swims in Greece for families and young children. Lifeguards are present during peak season.

What is the best month to visit Balos Lagoon?

June and September are the best months. Water is warm (22–25°C), the lagoon is calm, and crowds are manageable. July and August are warmer but extremely crowded — arrive early.

How deep is Balos Lagoon?

The lagoon is predominantly shallow. Knee to waist deep for most of the beach area, reaching chest depth only in the center. Maximum depth is 3–4 meters, increasing to 5–6 meters only at the western opening toward the sea.

Can you visit Balos Lagoon by boat?

Yes. Daily boat tours depart from Kissamos harbor (approximately 30 minutes by boat). This is a good alternative if you prefer not to drive the winding mountain road.
