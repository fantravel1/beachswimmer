Seitan Limania Beach

White cliffs, turquoise lagoon, and an iconic cliff entry into calm Mediterranean water.

91 SwimScore™

The Swim

Seitan Limania is a marvel of geology and water quality. Towering white cliffs frame a sheltered cove with impossibly clear water and minimal currents. The beach itself is small and rocky, but swimmers access the water via a dramatic 10-meter cliff jump or careful ladder descent into water that's 6+ meters deep.

Best swim: May through September. The water is warmest in August (25°C) and visibility peaks in early June before summer tourism peaks.

What Makes Seitan Limania Exceptional

Dramatic Natural Beauty

The 300-meter white-limestone cliffs that frame this cove create one of Greece's most striking coastal landscapes. The water clarity reveals the light-colored stone on the sea floor, amplifying the turquoise hue.

Calm Water — Even in Summer

The cove's natural breakwater effect means this beach stays calm even when nearby open coastlines are rough. Wave heights rarely exceed 0.5 meters, making it safe for intermediate swimmers.

Incredible Clarity

Seitan Limania consistently ranks among the clearest beaches in the Mediterranean. On good days, visibility exceeds 30 meters — perfect for snorkeling and underwater photography.

Off-Season Solitude

Most tourists arrive in July and August. Visit in June or September for world-class water with 10% of the crowds.

Access & Getting There

Nearest Airport: Chania International (CHQ) — 30 km away, 45 minutes by car

From Chania City: Drive west toward Kaliviani, then follow signs to Seitan Limania. The final 2 km is unpaved but passable in standard vehicles.

Parking: Free lot above the beach. Often full by 10 AM in peak season.

Best Time: May–June or September–early October for warm water and manageable crowds.
