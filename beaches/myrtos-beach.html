Myrtos Beach

Dramatic white-pebble beach overlooked by 300-meter red cliffs and impossibly blue Ionian water.

85 SwimScore™

The Swim

Myrtos is one of Greece's most visually striking beaches: 2 km of white pebbles with turquoise Ionian water backed by dramatic red-limestone cliffs. The beach is famous for its aesthetic appeal, but swimmers will appreciate the consistently calm conditions, excellent water clarity, and surprisingly good entry for a pebble beach.

Best swims: May through September. The water warms to 25°C by August. Pebbles are large but comfortable with aqua shoes.

What Makes Myrtos Unforgettable

Unmatched Visual Drama

The 300-meter red cliffs towering directly above the beach create a backdrop that feels almost unreal. Swimming here, you feel simultaneously isolated and observed by the landscape.

Consistent Calm

Despite being on the Ionian coast, Myrtos rarely experiences significant swells. The beach faces north and is protected by Zakynthos island offshore, creating exceptional swimming conditions.

Accessible Crowds

Unlike remote beaches, Myrtos is easy to reach. A well-maintained road and ample parking mean you can swim here without expedition logistics. Perfect for swimmers prioritizing quality conditions over solitude.

Excellent Infrastructure

Beach bars, showers, and amenities make it easy to spend a full day. Nearby towns offer good accommodation and restaurants.

Access & Logistics

Nearest Airport: Kefalonia (EFL) — 35 km away, 50 minutes by car

From Kefalonia Town: Head north on the main road. Myrtos is well-signed (about 30 km, 40 minutes). Excellent asphalt road and large car park.

Parking: €1–2 per day. Arrives early in July/August (by 8 AM) for guaranteed spots.

Facilities: Beach bars, umbrellas, showers, and tavernas along the shoreline.

Best Time: May–June or September–October for warm water with manageable crowds.
