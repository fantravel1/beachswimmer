Platja de Bol Nou

Intimate golden-sand cove hidden beneath red cliffs on one of Spain's most beautiful coastlines.

82 SwimScore™

The Swim

Platja de Bol Nou is a pocket cove with about 150 meters of fine golden sand and crystal-clear water sheltered by dramatic orange-red cliffs on three sides. The beach remains uncrowded because it's accessed via a steep stone staircase carved into the rock (about 60 steps). The effort keeps it quiet but manageable for any swimmers with moderate fitness.

Swim season: May through September. Water is warmest in August (24°C) and clarity peaks in June before summer algae blooms elsewhere on Costa Brava.

Why Bol Nou Stands Out

Naturally Selective Access

The 60-step staircase is the secret. It's not difficult, but it filters out swimmers looking for beach bar convenience. Result: a genuinely quiet cove within striking distance of coastal towns.

Red-Rock Geology

The distinctive orange-red cliffs are unique to this section of Costa Brava. They create an intimate swimming environment and dramatic scenery without the remoteness of more isolated beaches.

Consistent Calmness

The cove's shape and orientation mean it stays protected from Atlantic swells that affect nearby Costa Brava beaches. Ideal for swimmers avoiding rough conditions.

Near Excellent Infrastructure

Tossa de Mar is a charming seaside town 10 minutes away with excellent restaurants, hotels, and train connections. You get quiet swimming with easy access to civilization.

Getting There

Nearest Airport: Barcelona (BCN) — 90 km away, 90 minutes by car or train

Train Option: Take the regional train (R11 or S1) from Barcelona to Tossa de Mar (about 90 minutes). This is the easiest option if traveling from Barcelona.

By Car: From Barcelona, follow the coast road (GI-682) toward Tossa de Mar. Drive through the old town and follow signs toward "Bol Nou" — there's a small car park at the top of the staircase.

Parking: Free lot (10–15 spaces). Often full by 10 AM in July/August. Arrive early or visit in June/September.

Best Time: May–June or September for warm water and light crowds.
