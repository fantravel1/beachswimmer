Cala Macarella

Secret turquoise cove surrounded by pine-covered cliffs and pristine Mediterranean water.

86 SwimScore™

The Swim

Cala Macarella is the textbook definition of a hidden Balearic cove. Just 300 meters of golden sand with a narrow entrance framed by dramatic red-rock cliffs and fragrant pine forest. The water is remarkably calm, with a gentle sandy slope and clarity that rivals famous beaches at a fraction of the crowds.

Unique feature: a submerged natural rock arch 15 meters offshore, popular with snorkelers. Perfect for intermediate swimmers seeking calm, clear water.

What Makes Cala Macarella Special

Genuinely Remote

Despite being on Menorca, Cala Macarella feels untouched. The rough access road keeps day-trippers away. Most days, you'll share the cove with fewer than 50 swimmers.

Exceptional Water Quality

The combination of deep water, minimal runoff, and strong currents keeps the cove pristine. Water tests consistently show excellent clarity and zero pollution.

Rock Arch Snorkel

The natural underwater arch is a unique feature. Swim out about 15–20 minutes to explore. The seabed at the arch is 6–8 meters deep, surrounded by rock formations and small fish.

Dramatic Landscape

Red and orange rock cliffs rise directly from the water, contrasting with turquoise water and dense Mediterranean pine forest above.

Access & Logistics

Nearest Airport: Menorca (MAH) — 20 km away, 25 minutes by car

From Menorca Town: Head south toward Ciudadela. Follow signs to Cala Macarella (about 15 km, 20 minutes). The final 2 km is unpaved with large ruts — high-clearance or 4WD recommended.

Parking: Free lot (often full by 11 AM in summer). Arrive early or visit shoulder season.

Facilities: None. Bring water and snacks.

Best Time: May–June or September–October for warm water with light crowds.
